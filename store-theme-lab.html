Why this one works

Contrast (WCAG)

Targets: body text 4.5+, dim text 4.5 (3.0 acceptable for the big stencil sizes), prices 4.5+, button label 4.5+. Numbers are computed from the live swatches.

The rules in play

1. One field, one accent. 80 to 90 percent of the panel sits in a single low-chroma hue family; saturation is spent in exactly one place (the action gold). Reads as premium because it mirrors a scene lit by one source.

2. Temperature does the figure-ground. The world is warm. A cool field separates the panel with zero extra borders, and by simultaneous contrast it makes gold and ember physically glow.

3. Chromatic neutrals only. No pure gray, no pure black. Every neutral leans toward the theme hue, the way real material bounces ambient light. Pure gray reads unfinished and nobody can say why.

4. One direction of shadow drift. Within a theme, darker steps drift consistently warmer (firelight logic) or consistently cooler (night logic). Mixed drift is the invisible "something is off".

5. Signal hues are sacred. Red, amber, nominal green and info blue mean states. A theme may not spend them decoratively; the oxblood exhibit shows the veto.

6. Small saturated beats large saturated. A saturated color looks brighter than its measured luminance (Helmholtz-Kohlrausch), so the buy button and the money chip stay small and everything else stays quiet.
