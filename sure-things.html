Sure Things

A field guide to the things we measure to absurd precision, or lean on every single day, and still cannot explain.

Drop your phone and it falls. It has always fallen, for everyone, everywhere, every single time. You would call that proof. In the strict sense there is none, and stranger still, no one can tell you what gravity actually is.

Once you notice that gap, you find it everywhere. The list below is a collection of sure things: facts we have measured to eye-watering precision, or built our whole world on top of, and still cannot explain or prove. Two kinds of gap recur. Sometimes the proof is missing, because science never truly proves a universal law, it only fails to disprove it. More often the explanation is missing: we can write the thing down exactly and have no idea why it is true.

Every entry here is real and rock solid as a phenomenon. It is the understanding that runs out. Each is sourced; follow the links to go deeper.

Forty-two of them, in six shelves

01

The Cosmos

The biggest, best-measured things, owed to causes we cannot see.

Gravity

Pinned down to a change smaller than a ten-thousandth of a proton's width. The best-tested idea we own.

And still nobody can say why mass pulls on mass.

"No one has since given any machinery."Richard Feynman, Lectures on Physics

Dark Matter

Galaxies spin too fast for their visible mass. The missing pull is 27% of the universe, by Planck's accounting.

Not one particle of it has ever been detected. We know it only by its gravity.

"It is a strange and mysterious universe. But that's fun."Vera Rubin

Dark Energy

The universe's expansion is accelerating, pushed by a dark energy that is 68% of everything (2011 Nobel).

Our best theory of its value misses by 120 orders of magnitude.

"...a glaring embarrassment."Sean Carroll, on the cosmological constant

The Big Bang

The afterglow, the expansion, and the light elements fix the universe's age at 13.8 billion years (Planck).

The theory says nothing about what banged, or what came before.

"In its original form, the Big Bang theory never was a theory of the bang."Alan Guth, MIT

Why Anything Exists

Matter and antimatter should have wiped each other out into pure light. A surplus of one part in a billion survived (CERN).

That leftover is every star and planet, and where the imbalance came from is unexplained.

"...this kind of quantity is only 10⁻¹⁸ in the Standard Model prediction."LHCb, Physics World

Something, Not Nothing

Even empty space is measurably not empty: the Casimir effect pushes bare metal plates together, confirmed to within a percent.

Why there is anything at all defeats every explanation, since each one starts by assuming something already exists.

"Why is there something rather than nothing? For nothing is simpler and easier than something."G. W. Leibniz, 1714

Cosmic Rays

In 1991 a single subatomic particle hit Utah carrying 48 joules, the punch of a thrown baseball packed into one proton (the Oh-My-God particle).

Decades on, nothing in the sky in its direction is powerful enough to have made it.

"That's the mystery of this, what the heck is going on?"John Matthews, Telescope Array

The Hubble Tension

Our two best ways to clock the universe's expansion give 73.0 and 67.4 km/s per megaparsec, each measured to about a percent.

They disagree at more than five sigma, and nobody knows whether it is an error or new physics.

"This is in more than 5σ tension with Planck's prediction."CERN Courier, 2024

02

Matter & Motion

Everyday physics, written to many decimals, working for reasons unknown.

Time

An optical lattice clock keeps time so steadily it would drift by one second in 30 billion years.

Yet physics cannot say what time is, why it runs one way, or whether the future is even real.

"The past is set in stone while the future can still be altered... all because of entropy."Sean Carroll, Scientific American

Quantum Mechanics

It predicts the electron's magnetism to more than ten digits, and runs every microchip on Earth.

Nobody agrees what the wavefunction is, or what "measurement" even does.

"I think I can safely say that nobody understands quantum mechanics."Richard Feynman, 1965

Entanglement

The 2022 Nobel confirmed entangled particles really do break local realism, and we already build cryptography on them.

How two particles act as one thing across any distance is unexplained.

"I'm totally confused. I have no idea what all of this means."John Clauser, Nobel laureate, Space.com

A Magic Number

The fine-structure constant, near 1/137, sets the size of atoms and the color of light; we know it to a part per billion.

No theory explains why it has that value.

"A magic number that comes to us with no understanding by man."Richard Feynman, QED, 1985

Inertia

Every kilogram resists a push exactly as F = ma says; inertial and gravitational mass match to two parts in 10¹⁵.

Why matter resists being pushed at all has no accepted explanation.

"Inertia originates in a kind of interaction between bodies."Albert Einstein, letter to Ernst Mach, 1913

Turbulence

We model it well enough to build jets and predict their drag to a few percent.

There is still no theory of it from first principles; proving the equations behave is a $1,000,000 unclaimed prize.

"Nobody in physics has really been able to analyze it mathematically satisfactorily."Richard Feynman, Lectures on Physics

Glass

Glass carries the whole internet, drawn to within a micron of target.

How a cooling liquid turns rigid without crystallizing is a famous unsolved problem.

"The deepest and most interesting unsolved problem in solid state theory."Philip W. Anderson, Science, 1995

Superconductivity

Every MRI and the LHC run on wire that carries current with zero resistance.

For the high-temperature kind, found in 1986, there is still no agreed theory after forty years.

"In 30 years people will still say that the theory is not understood."A.-M. Tremblay, Physics Today

Static Electricity

Rub two materials together and charge reliably builds; it is the oldest known electrical effect, written up by the Greeks around 585 BC.

After all that time there is still no agreed account of how the charge moves, or even which way it should go.

Lightning

Lightning strikes the Earth about three million times a day.

And the fields inside storm clouds measure ten times too weak to start the spark. How it begins is unsolved.

"The most energetic process on the planet... and we have no idea how it works."Brian Hare, Nautilus

Sonoluminescence

Aim sound into water and it collapses tiny bubbles that flash with light, from gas heated to thousands of degrees, in under a billionth of a second.

Exactly how a bubble turns sound into light remains debated.

The Bicycle

A moving riderless bicycle steers itself upright and recovers from a shove, shown in a 2011 Science paper.

The two textbook explanations, the gyroscope and the trail, are both unnecessary; the authors built a stable bike with neither.

"We show that neither effect is necessary for self-stability."Kooijman et al., Science, 2011

03

The Living World

Life is easy to start, hard to define, and no one knows why it must end.

The Origin of Life

A 1953 spark made the building blocks of life from nothing but raw gas. The chemistry is easy to start.

How ordinary matter first crossed into something that could copy and evolve itself is unknown.

"We're just feeling our way around in the dark, trying to see where there might be a path."Jack Szostak, Nobel laureate, Harvard Magazine

What Life Even Is

We sequence a genome in hours and tell a cat from a rock instantly.

Yet no definition of life survives the edge cases; one survey gathered 123 of them, agreeing on nothing.

"You can't rigorously define life because it's not a scientific term to begin with."Gerald Joyce, NASA Astrobiology

Aging

Aging is not a law of nature. A hydra shows almost no decline for years, and a Greenland shark can live 400 years.

Why most of us age, then, has no agreed cause, only competing theories.

Sleep

Every animal with a nervous system sleeps, and rats kept fully awake died within 11 to 32 days.

Science still cannot agree what sleep is for.

"If sleep does not serve an absolutely vital function, then it is the biggest mistake the evolutionary process has ever made."Allan Rechtschaffen, University of Chicago

Why Sex Exists

Sex carries a brutal twofold cost: an asexual lineage should outbreed it two to one. Yet nearly all complex life reproduces sexually.

Why sex wins anyway is unsettled.

"The queen of problems in evolutionary biology."Graham Bell, The Masterpiece of Nature, 1982

How Birds Find South

Robins and many other birds navigate by Earth's magnetic field, proven in cage experiments since the 1960s.

The actual sensor has never been found; the lead idea is a quantum reaction inside the eye.

"The primary sensory mechanism behind this remarkable feat is still unclear."Hore & Mouritsen, Annual Review of Biophysics, 2016

04

The Body

Medicine that works without knowing why, on hardware we never designed.

Anesthesia

We switch consciousness off for surgery hundreds of millions of times a year, safely, and have since 1846.

After 175 years, no one can fully say how it erases awareness.

"That's been a great mystery for about 170 years. The straightforward answer is no one really knows."Paul Myles, anesthesiologist, ScienceAlert

Drugs That Just Work

Lithium has steadied bipolar disorder since 1949, and we swallow acetaminophen by the billion.

Regulators demand proof a drug works, not proof of how, and for these the mechanism is still unknown.

"The specific mechanisms by which lithium exerts its mood-stabilizing effects are not well understood."Malhi et al., CNS Drugs, 2013

The Placebo Effect

It is so strong every drug must beat it. In one trial, sugar pills taken knowingly still gave 59% vs 35% relief.

Why a pill you know is fake still works has no settled explanation.

"The psychological mechanisms underlying the observed effectiveness of open label placebo are unclear."Kaptchuk & Miller, BMJ, 2018

Memory

We can now tag the exact brain cells of a single memory in a mouse and switch it back on with light (2012).

And still no one can say what a memory physically is, or how the brain writes it down.

Smell

Your nose runs on about 400 kinds of receptor and sorts an enormous range of odors.

Yet no one can predict what a molecule will smell like from its shape; near-identical molecules can smell completely different.

A Bundle of Quirks

Universal human traits we all share: yawning, blushing, hiccups, tears, laughter, and being ~90% right-handed.

None of them has a settled purpose. Darwin could not explain the blush, and neither can we.

"Blushing is the most peculiar and the most human of all expressions."Charles Darwin, 1872

05

The Mind

The one thing you know first-hand, and can prove to no one.

Consciousness

A 2023 system can reconstruct the image you are looking at from a brain scan with striking accuracy.

And nothing in neuroscience explains why all that processing feels like anything from the inside.

"There is a whir of information-processing, but there is also a subjective aspect."David Chalmers, on the hard problem, 1995

Free Will

Law, morality, and your whole sense of self rest on the feeling that you choose freely.

Yet brain signals can predict a "free" choice up to half a second before you feel you decide, and no one can settle what that means.

"Put all the scientific results together... and there's no room for free will."Robert Sapolsky, Determined, 2023

Other Minds

You have direct access to exactly one mind: your own.

That anyone else is awake inside, rather than a perfect look-alike, is a reasonable bet, never a proof.

Whether This Is Real

Everything you have ever known arrives through your senses.

And from Descartes' demon to the simulation argument, no sensory evidence can rule out that all of it is a flawless illusion.

"I shall suppose that some malicious, powerful, cunning demon has done all he can to deceive me."Rene Descartes, Meditations, 1641

06

Logic & the Machine

The limits of proof, the nature of chance, and the tools we built but cannot read.

That the Sun Will Rise

NASA can place the Moon a thousand years from now to within a few meters.

But that the future will resemble the past at all cannot be justified by logic or experience. It is why nothing is ever truly proven.

"That the sun will not rise tomorrow is no less intelligible than the affirmation that it will rise."David Hume, 1748

Truths That Can't Be Proven

Mathematics is the home of certainty; in 1931, at just 25, Kurt Godel proved his incompleteness theorems.

Any consistent system big enough for arithmetic holds true statements it can never prove, including its own consistency.

Why Math Fits the World

Pure math, dreamed up with no use in mind, keeps describing the universe exactly. Dirac's 1928 equation predicted antimatter four years before anyone saw it.

No one can say why abstract math should fit reality so well.

"A wonderful gift which we neither understand nor deserve."Eugene Wigner, on the unreasonable effectiveness of mathematics, 1960

True Randomness

We stake modern cryptography on it; a NIST beacon pours out certified random bits from quantum measurements.

Yet no test can ever certify a sequence as truly random, and quantum outcomes are, as far as we can tell, uncaused.

"True randomness is something that nothing in the universe can predict in advance."Krister Shalm, NIST, 2025

P versus NP

Modern cryptography, and much of the internet, assumes some problems are easy to check but hard to solve.

Whether that is even true, whether P = NP, is unproven, and carries a $1,000,000 prize.

"If it is easy to check that a solution to a problem is correct, is it also easy to solve the problem?"Clay Mathematics Institute

Why Deep Learning Works

The neural networks behind today's AI ace benchmark after benchmark; AlphaFold predicts protein shapes well enough to share a 2024 Nobel.

And no one, including the people who build them, can fully explain why training them works.

"No existing theory can guarantee that... gradient descent... can converge to the global minimum."Simon Du, Quanta, 2021

Notice what the last shelf ends on. The machine that drew every picture on this page, and helped write it, is the newest sure thing of all: it works, spectacularly, and no one can fully say why. We are back where we started, holding something that plainly works and cannot be explained.

That is not a defect in the list. It is the view from the edge of what we know, and the edge is closer, and friendlier, than it looks. None of these things is fake or in doubt. The phenomenon is always real. It is the why, or the final proof, that runs out, and that running-out is where every interesting question still lives.

A note on honesty: "unexplained" here means there is no scientific consensus, not that there are no ideas. Many of these have several competing theories and active research; a few, like the limits of proof or other minds, are matters of logic that no experiment can close. Every claim links to its source. Quotes are verbatim and attributed; where a phenomenon is contested, the wording leans toward "no agreed explanation" rather than "no idea at all."
