Which mobile chart fix?

The eight data figures in The Other Hours are built on a 700-unit-wide viewBox. On a 375px phone the SVG shrinks to about 287px (a 0.41x scale), so 10 to 13px SVG labels render at roughly 4.5 to 6.7 real pixels. Too small to read. Two of the eight (the ranked levers and the cold-plunge meters) are HTML, not SVG, and reflow fine, so they are left out. The six SVG charts below are the problem.

Each chart sits in a fixed 375px phone frame, so what you see here is what a phone shows, whatever width your screen is. Flip between the three fixes:

Now is today's broken rendering. Scroll is the site's existing .scrollx pattern (already used on three charts in Big Enough): the chart holds full size and the frame scrolls sideways, so text lands at 8 to 10px, but you trade the at-a-glance shape for a swipe. Bigger enlarges the labels in place with no scroll, so the shape stays glanceable, but because the viewBox has to stay 700 wide (or desktop breaks), the labels top out around 7 to 9px and the dense charts need hand-tuning.

smallest label on screen: measuring...

1 / Seven-hour floor

A U-curve. The shape carries even when tiny, but the axis text does not.

2 / What disconnection costs

Horizontal bars with named rows. Roomy, so bigger labels fit cleanly.

3 / The curve that wasn't there

Two lines plus floating annotations. The lines read shrunk; the notes do not.

4 / The blood-pressure leak

Text lives inside the bars. The narrow bottom bar is where Bigger has to redesign, not just resize.

5 / How dim your day is

Long left-side labels. In Bigger they collide with the frame edge unless shortened or the baseline shifts.

6 / The sauna numbers

Three fat vertical bars, lots of white space. Bigger has the easiest job here.

Measured target: at least 9 to 10 real pixels of label height at 375px. The readout at the top reports the smallest text on screen for the current mode. Once you pick, the winner gets applied to the six charts here plus the same defect in Still Moving (7 charts) and What You're Supposed to Eat (5 charts), and this lab page gets deleted.
