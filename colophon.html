Colophon

A page about the page. Every color, font, and moving part this site is built from, laid out in the open and labeled by who made it.

A field guide to itself / made here, or borrowed / every part labeled

This is the workshop. If you have read a few of these posts and wondered what they are actually made of, here is the whole kit, laid out on the bench and named.

The site runs on a small, fixed set of parts. One background color that never changes, a warm palette tuned around it, three typefaces and nothing else, and a few dozen building blocks that every post is assembled from. There is no framework here, no design library you could download, no theme. It is a closed set, drawn for this one site, and this page is just an honest inventory of it.

And honesty is the point of the page, so it keeps one plain distinction. Next to each part is a small tag. One of two:

Made here

It exists only on this site. Built for this one job and used nowhere else. Most of what follows is this: the palette, every component, the small machinery around the edges.

Borrowed

I did not make the thing itself. I chose it and wired it in: the typefaces, the visit counters, a standard browser trick or two. The few borrowed pieces are the ones worth being upfront about.

One more thing before the tour. Almost every component below began life as a row of competing options on a throwaway test page, and what you see here is the single version that won. So this is not a catalog of everything that was tried. It is the set of decisions that stuck.

Chapter one

One color, and a palette tuned around it

The background is a single dark forest green, and it is locked. Everything else on the site exists to sit on top of it without ever fighting it.

The palette was tuned in OKLCH, a way of describing color that tracks how the eye actually reads lightness, so the steps up the ramp feel even instead of lurching. Nothing is a neutral gray and nothing is pure white. The darks are warm and green, the lights are cream, and the one everyday accent is a dusty gold. There is a rose note held in reserve for the single most important link on a page, and a small set of muted accents that posts reach for when they need to color code something.

the neutral ramp Made here

Dark to light in one smooth curve. The hue glides from green toward cream as it brightens; the chroma stays low the whole way. --bg is the locked background. The names in gold are the actual CSS variables a post uses.

Chapter two

Three typefaces, and not a fourth

Every word on the site is set in one of three faces. Headlines in a serif, body text in a familiar sans, and anything that behaves like data in a monospace.

Here is the borrowed part, said plainly: I did not draw these letters. Century Supra is a serif in the Century family, the kind of face you find set in old books. Segoe UI is Microsoft's interface font, loaded first as the one your own machine probably already has and only downloaded if it does not. Commit Mono is a free monospace built for code. What is mine is the casting, which face does which job, at what size, against this green. The letters are borrowed; the typesetting is made here.

the three faces Borrowed

All three are self hosted, so the site pulls from no font network and nothing tracks you through them. They are reached in CSS as --font-heading, --font-body, and --font-mono.

And a close

How it is all held together

There is no build step and no server of my own. Every page is a single hand written HTML file that links one stylesheet, loads three fonts, and runs a little plain JavaScript.

You can hit View Source on any page and read the entire thing, top to bottom, with nothing minified or hidden. The files are served straight from GitHub's free hosting, and pushing to the main branch is the whole of the deploy. No pipeline, no dashboard, no framework to keep fed.

One more honest note, since honesty is the whole frame of this page. I had a great deal of help building all of this. Most of the code was written with an AI coding assistant, working alongside me one small piece at a time, and there is a whole post that takes that apart commit by commit, including a chart of which model wrote which page, if you want the long version.

That is the site, with its back panel off. Thank you for being curious enough to look inside.

Set in Century Supra, Segoe UI, and Commit Mono, on a locked forest green. Built in the open.
