How you get in

No login button lives on the public site, ever. A stranger sees a completely normal page. These are the discreet ways the edit controls can appear, but only on a device that has already unlocked once (the harmless be_owner flag). Pick the one that feels right; clicking any of them still asks for your password.

Option A is what is live right now (js/owner-edit.js). Tell me which you want and I will swap it in. Each mock below is interactive.

D. Nothing visible

No control at all. You reach the editor only by typing /edit.html or a secret keyboard gesture. The page stays pixel-for-pixel normal even for you.

Maximum restraint. The cost: nothing reminds you it is there, and quick edits take an extra step.

Getting in on a brand-new device

  • Shipped: visit /edit.html (a short alias that lands you on the password screen). Works on any device, no flag needed.
  • Optional gesture: I can wire a quiet keyboard gesture (for example, typing a secret word on any page when nothing is focused) to open the editor. Tell me the word and I will add it.
  • Optional secret click: a triple-click on the footer copyright mark. Invisible to anyone who does not know it is there.

Once you unlock on a device, the flag is set and your chosen affordance appears from then on. "Forget this device" clears the flag (and the in-memory key) for a shared or public computer.
