For the "great books, made learn-from-able" series. Engine A (translation stack),

the foundation for the later New Testament post, which will build on it.

## 1. The distinctively Jewish frame (the pitch)

The post is the Tanakh read **as Judaism reads it**, not the Christian "Old Testament."
The difference is not the books (mostly the same text); it is the lens. Five load-bearing
differences drive the post:

1. **Tanakh, not "Old Testament."** TaNaKh is an acronym for the three parts: **Torah**
   (teaching, the Five Books), **Nevi'im** (Prophets), **Ketuvim** (Writings). "Old
   Testament" is a Christian frame that casts the whole thing as a prelude to a sequel. The
   post never uses it except to name the contest.
2. **The written and Oral Torah are inseparable.** Jews hold that an oral tradition (the
   Mishnah, Talmud, Midrash, the commentators) was given alongside the written text and is
   how it is read. The text is never read alone. Hillel's "go and learn" is the hinge.
3. **Covenant and peoplehood (brit).** The story is a two-way binding deal between God and a
   people, beginning with Abraham (Genesis 12) and sealed at Sinai. It is national and
   particular before it is universal.
4. **Law as the content of the relationship (mitzvot, halakha).** "Torah" is closer to
   *teaching/instruction* than to *law*, and keeping the commandments is how the covenant is
   lived, not a legalism to be transcended.
5. **This-worldly ethics; near silence on the afterlife.** Reward and ruin land here, in the
   land, in the children, in history. "Choose life" (Deut 30) means this life. Job and
   Kohelet are kept precisely because they refuse a tidy afterlife resolution.

**The spine (diagnosis and prescription):** a people keeps straying from the covenant and
from justice; the repair is **return (teshuvah)**, **keeping the commandments**, and
**pursuing justice as a holy people**, in this world. The post is organized so the keystones
are the answers to those two questions.

Respect rules: never glib, never supersessionist, name the Jewish/Christian contest as a
teaching point rather than fake a neutral center. No Christ-typology, no original-sin
emphasis. These are the living scriptures of a people.

## 2. The keystones (17), why each is load-bearing, by section

The selection rule is **load-bearing, not popular.** Hillel's self-summary is the frame.

**Talmud (the Oral Torah frame)**
- **Hillel on one foot** (Bavli, Shabbat 31a). The tradition's own one-sentence summary, plus
  "the rest is commentary, go and learn," which states the method: never read alone.

**Torah**
- **Creation + image of God** (Gen 1:1-5, 26-27; 2:7). The bereshit grammar fork; *tzelem
  Elohim*, every human in God's image (Akiva's ground of human worth); *nefesh* not "soul."
- **Call of Abraham** (Gen 12:1-3). Where peoplehood and covenant begin.
- **The binding of Isaac** (Gen 22). The Akedah; *hineni*; read on Rosh Hashanah; the limit case.
- **The Name** (Ex 3:13-15). *Ehyeh asher ehyeh*; the unpronounced Tetragrammaton.
- **The Ten Words** (Ex 20:1-6). Jewish enumeration: "I am the LORD" is the first, a statement.
- **Love your neighbor** (Lev 19:1-2, 17-18). The Holiness Code; Akiva vs Ben Azzai.
- **The Shema** (Deut 6:4-9). The creed; "one" vs "alone"; commanded love.
- **Justice, justice** (Deut 16:18-20). *Tzedek tzedek tirdof*; the prophets' root word.
- **Choose life** (Deut 30:15-20). The clearest this-world statement; teshuvah and free choice.

**Nevi'im**
- **Seek justice** (Isaiah 1:16-17). The prophetic indictment: ethics over cult.
- **The young woman** (Isaiah 7:14). *almah*; the central Jewish/Christian translation contest.
- **Let justice roll down** (Amos 5:21-24). The thunderclap against hollow worship.
- **Do justice, love kindness** (Micah 6:6-8). The prophetic summary; *chesed*.

**Ketuvim**
- **Psalm 23.** Cadence showcase (KJV vs Alter); *nefesh* again ("my life," not "my soul").
- **Mere breath** (Ecclesiastes 1:2). *hevel*; the wisdom counterweight; ends back at the mitzvot.
- **Out of the whirlwind** (Job 38). Suffering with no afterlife payoff, kept in the canon anyway.

Fold-ins used in breakdowns, not as separate entries: Hosea 6:6 ("I desire kindness, not
sacrifice"), Proverbs 9:10 (fear of the LORD), Genesis 5:1 (Ben Azzai), Job 1:21.

## 3. Contested points, each verified

- **Bereshit grammar.** *B'reshit* is pointed with a shva, not a kamatz, marking a construct
  state ("in the beginning **of**"), so 1:1 reads as a dependent clause: "When God began to
  create." Rashi ties 1:1 to 1:3 (1:2 parenthetical); Ibn Ezra ties 1:1 to 1:2. Both deny
  that creation *ex nihilo* is forced by the grammar. NJPS, Alter, Fox all render the
  construct reading; KJV and JPS 1917 keep the absolute "In the beginning God created."
  *Verified (Sefaria; Wikipedia "Genesis 1:1"; Winebrenner Seminary analysis).*
- **Ehyeh asher ehyeh.** The verb is imperfect/future, "I will be," not the static Greek-
  flavored "I AM." Alter: "I-Will-Be-Who-I-Will-Be." Fox: "I will be-there howsoever I will
  be-there." *Verified (multiple).*
- **Decalogue enumeration.** Jewish tradition counts "I am the LORD your God, who brought you
  out of Egypt" (Ex 20:2) as the **first** of the Ten ("Ten Statements," *Aseret haDibrot*,
  *devarim* = words, not "commandments"). Makkot 24a: 611 mitzvot from Moses + the first two
  heard directly from God = 613. Most Christian counts treat 20:2 as a preface and begin at
  "no other gods," so the tens never align. *Verified (Makkot 24a via My Jewish Learning;
  Wikipedia "I am the Lord thy God").*
- **Akiva vs Ben Azzai.** On Lev 19:18, Rabbi Akiva: "Love your neighbor as yourself, this is
  a great principle (*klal gadol*) of the Torah." Ben Azzai: Genesis 5:1 ("the book of the
  generations of Adam... in the likeness of God He made him") is a *greater* principle,
  because it grounds the dignity of *every* human, not just one's own kind. *Verified
  (Genesis Rabbah 24:7; Sifra Kedoshim; JT Nedarim).*
- **almah vs betulah.** *Almah* (Isaiah 7:14) = young woman of marriageable age; *betulah* is
  the specific word for virgin. The Septuagint rendered it *parthenos* (virgin); Matthew 1:23
  quoted that as a virgin-birth prophecy; the KJV followed. Every Jewish translation: "the
  young woman." In context it is a near-term sign to King Ahaz during the Syro-Ephraimite war
  (~734 BCE). *Verified.*
- **nefesh.** Not the immortal Greek soul. The whole living, breathing creature/life. Alter
  renders Gen 2:7 "a living creature" and Ps 23 "my life He brings back," explicitly to avoid
  importing body/soul dualism. *Verified (Alter; Sefaria sheet on nefesh).*

## 4. Untranslatables featured

YHWH (the Name, written not spoken, read as *Adonai*, referred to as *Hashem*); torah
(teaching/instruction, not merely "law"); nefesh (life/being, not "soul"); tzedek /
tzedakah (justice / righteousness / charity, one root); hevel (breath/vapor, not "vanity");
chesed (loyal love/kindness/mercy); brit (covenant); teshuvah (return/repentance).

## 5. Translation landscape (each named in the post)

| Translation | Year | Character | Coverage in post |
|---|---|---|---|
| JPS 1917 | 1917 | first English Tanakh by a Jewish committee; archaic, KJV-like | all |
| NJPS | 1985 | the modern Jewish standard; idiomatic, scholarly | all |
| Robert Alter | 1996-2018 | the literary landmark; concrete, rhythm-first (**featured**) | featured passages |
| Everett Fox | 1995 | echoes Hebrew sound/syntax; hyphenated, "YHWH" | Torah only |
| Koren Jerusalem | 1962 | Jewish synagogue Bible (Harold Fisch); Hebraized names | all |
| KJV | 1611 | the famous English cadence (the Christian-tradition foil) | all |
| Young's Literal | 1898 | hyper-literal; "Jehovah," bracketed supplied words | all |

Alter is not online as one clean machine-readable text, so he is quoted only where his exact
wording was verified against a published source: in the stack on Creation, Psalm 23, and
Ecclesiastes 1:2, and named for his distinctive choices (welter and waste, I-Will-Be-Who-I-
Will-Be, human/humus, living creature, vale of death's shadow, your being) across the
breakdowns.
