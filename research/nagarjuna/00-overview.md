## The spine (diagnosis -> prescription), verified
- **Disease:** we grasp things and the self as having **svabhava** (fixed, independent essence).
  That projection is the root delusion; it drives grasping, action, and suffering.
- **Cure:** see **sunyata** (emptiness). Nothing has svabhava, because everything arises
  dependently (**pratityasamutpada**). Emptiness is NOT nothingness; it is the absence of
  independent essence, which is exactly what lets things change, cause, and be freed. A thing
  with a fixed essence could never change. (24.18-19, ch 24 broadly.)

## Keystone verses (all Garfield-verbatim in 01)
- Dedication: the **eight negations** + homage to the teacher of dependent arising.
- **24.18-19**: dependent arising = emptiness = a dependent designation = the middle way; and
  nothing not-dependent exists, so nothing not-empty exists. THE keystone.
- **24.8-10**: the two truths (conventional / ultimate); without the conventional the ultimate
  can't be taught.
- **Ch 2 (2.1, 2.8, 2.25)**: motion deconstructed (goer/going/gone) = the reductio method.
- **13.8**: the emptiness of emptiness; emptiness as a view is incurable.
- **25.19-20**: not the slightest difference between samsara and nirvana.
- Plus **18.5** (the chain prapanca -> grasping -> suffering, stopped by emptiness; the payoff)
  and **25.24** (the book unsays itself: no dharma was ever taught).

## Sidebar stacks (only where translators genuinely diverge)
1. The **eight negations** (Garfield / Batchelor-literal / Sanskrit). Batchelor's secular voice:
   "contingency" / "to ease fixations."
2. **24.18** six translators (Robinson, Inada, Sprung, Kalupahana, Garfield, Siderits-Katsura).
   The split is on prajnaptir upadaya. The centerpiece.
3. **The three words**: svabhava (essence / inherent existence / intrinsic nature /
   self-existence / own-being), sunyata (emptiness, NOT "the void"), prapanca (conceptual
   construction / phenomenal extension / fixations / objectification).
- NOTE: 25.19 is the rare verse where everyone agrees almost word-for-word; say so, don't stack.

## Honesty flags baked into the post
- The **chariot** is Nagasena's (Milindapanha) and Chandrakirti's (sevenfold), NOT in the MMK;
  Nagarjuna's own version is **fire and fuel** (ch 10). Attribute it as the tradition's handle.
- **Svatantrika/Prasangika** is a later (6th-7th c.), retrospective Tibetan label; MMK predates
  it and proceeds by reductio.
- **Streng** verbatim verses unavailable open-web; use him for term choices only.
- "Form is emptiness": MMK is the ARGUMENT under the Heart Sutra's slogan (Prajnaparamita
  asserts, Nagarjuna proves); don't overclaim the relation.
- Adversarial answers kept visible: nihilism charge (24.11 snake), motion-as-wordgames steelman,
  "essence" imports Aristotle, dependent-origination-broadened, 25.19 nuance.
