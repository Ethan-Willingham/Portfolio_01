# Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* — the five modern (in-copyright) translations

Keystone lines verified for the translation-comparison post. The five editions:

- **Hays** — Gregory Hays, 2002, Modern Library (the punchy modern)
- **Hammond** — Martin Hammond, 2006, Penguin Classics
- **Hard** — Robin Hard, 2011, Oxford World's Classics (some sites tag the 1997 ed.; wording often identical)
- **Staniforth** — Maxwell Staniforth, 1964, Penguin Classics
- **Farquharson** — A. S. L. Farquharson, 1944, Oxford (literal)

## Scope / fair-use note (important)

All five are in copyright. We quote only ONE short, famous keystone line per
passage per translator, each clearly attributed (translator + book.section), as
fair-use quotation for criticism/comparison (the way Daily Stoic does). This is
NOT a reconstruction or substantial reproduction. For full side-by-side renderings
use the public-domain translations (Long 1862, Casaubon 1634, Hutcheson/Moor 1742).

## Method / confidence

- **Primary anchor for most lines: WIST Quotations (wist.info).** WIST publishes
  the same section across many *named, dated* translators side by side, quoting the
  printed editions. It is the single most reliable translator-attributing source found.
- **conf: high** = WIST PLUS at least one independent source that also names the
  translator/edition (or two independent non-WIST sources agree).
- **conf: medium** = WIST only (or one strong source only). Wording is from a
  real attributed source but a clean second corroboration was not found. Usable,
  but mark in the post or re-check against a print copy if you want high confidence.
- **conf: low / NOT FOUND** = not confirmed from any translator-naming source. Do
  not quote.
- Punctuation is reproduced as printed. Some editions use en/em dashes; render with
  the site's dash convention but the wording must stay verbatim.

Hays lines already verified in `hays.md` (5.20, 4.7, 9.6) are carried forward and
not re-litigated here; this file resolves several Hays lines `hays.md` left open
(notably 5.16, 10.16, 9.30) and adds the other four translators.

### 2.1 — the morning meditation (you will meet the meddler, the ungrateful, the arrogant)

- Hays: "When you wake up in the morning, tell yourself: The people I deal with today will be meddling, ungrateful, arrogant, dishonest, jealous, and surly." (src: https://wist.info/marcus-aureleus/74557/ ; corrob: https://gregorybsadler.substack.com / themarginalian style reposts show identical wording; conf: high)
- Hammond: "Say to yourself first thing in the morning: today I shall meet people who are meddling, ungrateful, aggressive, treacherous, malicious, unsocial." (src: https://wist.info/marcus-aureleus/74557/ ; conf: medium — WIST-only)
- Hard: "Say to yourself at the start of the day, I shall meet with meddling, ungrateful, violent, treacherous, envious, and unsociable people." (src: https://wist.info/marcus-aureleus/74557/ ; conf: medium — WIST-only)
- Staniforth: "Begin each day by telling yourself: Today I shall be meeting with interference, ingratitude, insolence, disloyalty, ill will, and selfishness" (src: https://wist.info/marcus-aureleus/74557/ ; corrob: https://studylib.net/doc/26975267/ (Staniforth excerpt) ; conf: high)
- Farquharson: "Say to yourself in the early morning: I shall meet to-day inquisitive, ungrateful, violent, treacherous, envious, uncharitable men." (src: https://wist.info/marcus-aureleus/74557/ ; conf: medium — WIST-only)

### 5.16 — the soul/mind takes its color from its thoughts

- Hays: "The things you think about determine the quality of your mind. Your soul takes on the color of your thoughts." (src: https://wist.info/marcus-aureleus/43042/ ; corrob: https://www.goodreads.com / multiple Hays-attributed reposts; conf: high)
  - DISCREPANCY to flag: one reliable Hays source (michaeldmcgill.com notes) prints the SECOND sentence as "Your soul takes on the **quality** of your thoughts" (i.e. "quality" twice), whereas WIST and most reposts print "...takes on the **color** of your thoughts." The Modern Library print text reads "color" (the "quality/quality" version looks like a transcription slip). Use "...takes on the color of your thoughts." If quoting the doubled sentence, re-check a print copy.
- Hammond: "Your mind will take on the character of your most frequent thoughts: souls are dyed by thoughts." (src: https://wist.info/marcus-aureleus/43042/ ; corrob: widely quoted as Hammond/Penguin 2006 e.g. azquotes & Penguin listing; conf: high)
- Hard: "As are your habitual conceptions, so will your mind be also; for the soul takes its colouring from its conceptions." (src: https://wist.info/marcus-aureleus/43042/ ; conf: medium — WIST-only)
- Staniforth: "Your mind will be like its habitual thoughts; for the soul becomes dyed with the colour of its thoughts." (src: https://wist.info/marcus-aureleus/43042/ ; corrob: "The soul becomes dyed with the colour of its thoughts" widely attributed to Staniforth/Penguin, e.g. https://www.goodreads.com/quotes/227344 ; conf: high)
- Farquharson: "As are your repeated imaginations so will your mind be, for the soul is dyed by its imaginations." (src: https://wist.info/marcus-aureleus/43042/ ; corrob: https://www.buboquote.com/en/quote/8559 (cites Farquharson 1944 Wikisource) ; conf: high)

## APOCRYPHA (protect the post — confirm these are NOT verbatim in any of the five)

### "You have power over your mind, not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength."

- **Verdict: APOCRYPHAL / fabricated.** Not a verbatim line in Hays, Hammond, Hard,
  Staniforth, Farquharson, or any standard translation. It is a loose modern
  paraphrase, possibly distilled from the general Stoic theme of 12.22 / 4.3
  (that disturbance comes from our own opinions).
- It has even been misattributed to Einstein, and online hunts (Reddit, Quora)
  turn up no source text. Treat it as a "victim of cumulative misattribution."
- Sources: https://www.goodreads.com/quotes/190580 (where it circulates, community-added, unverified) ; discussion that it is not in Meditations: https://medium.com/@zga/you-have-power-over-your-mind-not-outside-events-realize-this-and-you-will-find-strength-f17f75f03180 ; general fake-quote context: https://www.thelies.agency/blog/marcus-aurelius-misquote (HTTP 401 to the fetcher but indexed as a case study of MA misquotes).
- conf (that it is apocryphal): high.

### "Everything we hear is an opinion, not a fact. Everything we see is a perspective, not the truth."

- **Verdict: APOCRYPHAL / fabricated.** Donald Robertson (author of *How to Think
  Like a Roman Emperor*) states flatly: "That's a fake quote." It is described as
  "by far the most prolific quote misattributed to Marcus Aurelius."
- Where it really comes from: it appears to be a very loose expansion/mistranslation
  of **Meditations 4.3** ("everything is opinion," "the universe is change; life is
  opinion"). The fabricated version reads as relativism ("anything goes"), which the
  real Stoics would reject — a tell that it is not Marcus.
- Sources (fact-checks that call it fake): https://checkyourfact.com/2019/10/15/fact-check-marcus-aurelius-opinion-fact-perspective-truth-quote/ (quotes Robertson "fake quote") ; https://modernstoicism.com/did-the-stoics-really-say-that-by-thomas-colligan/ ; circulation (community-added, unverified): https://www.goodreads.com/quotes/73368 .
- conf (that it is apocryphal): high.

### Bonus apocrypha guard (frequently mislabeled as a MODERN edition)

These famous wordings are **public-domain (Long 1862 / Casaubon / Haines)**, NOT
Hays/Hammond/Hard/Staniforth/Farquharson. Do not present them as a modern edition:

- 6.6 "The best revenge is to be unlike him who performed the injury." = **Long**.
- 7.59 "Look within. Within is the fountain of good..." = **Long** (Hays' real 7.59 is the "Dig deep" rendering).
- 4.7 "Take away thy opinion, and then there is taken away the complaint 'I have been harmed'..." = **Long / Haines**.

## Source reliability ranking (for the post's "how we checked" note, if wanted)

1. **wist.info** — best: names translator + year + book.section, quotes the printed
   editions, shows them side by side. Primary anchor here.
2. **dailystoic.com**, **themarginalian.org**, **michaeldmcgill.com (Hays notes)**,
   **vreeman.com (Hays full text)** — reliable for Hays specifically, usually
   attributed.
3. **goodreads quote pages / azquotes / quotefancy** — corroboration ONLY; many are
   community-added and unverified, and frequently DROP or MISLABEL the translator.
   Use only to confirm wording already anchored by WIST or a named-edition source.
4. **ianchadwick.com "Seven Faces of Marcus Aurelius"** — good multi-edition
   comparison (Long/Staniforth/Hicks/Hays/Hard/Waterfield) but its worked passages
   (8.56, 6.21, etc.) don't overlap most of our keystones, and it omits Hammond and
   Farquharson.
