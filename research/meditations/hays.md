# Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* — Gregory Hays (2002, Modern Library)

**Scope note (important).** This translation is in copyright. For the
translation-comparison post we quote only a few short, famous keystone lines
from Hays, each clearly attributed (translator + book.section), as fair-use
quotation for criticism/comparison. We are NOT reconstructing or aggregating a
substantial portion of the Hays text from scattered web fragments. For breadth
in the post, use the public-domain translations (George Long 1862, Meric
Casaubon 1634, Hutcheson/Moor 1742) for full side-by-side renderings, and use
Hays only for these short attributed lines plus a link to the Modern Library
edition.

Each line below was corroborated across at least two independent sources that
explicitly attribute the wording to Hays.

---

### 5.20
"The impediment to action advances action. What stands in the way becomes the way."
src: https://www.michaeldmcgill.com/notes/meditations-marcus-aurelius-gregory-hays-translation/ ; corroborated-by: https://www.translatum.gr/forum/index.php?topic=414700.0 (cites Hays alongside the Greek) ; confidence: high
note: one secondary source mistypes the first clause as plural ("The impediments to action advance action"); the singular form above is the corroborated reading.

### 4.7
"Choose not to be harmed — and you won't feel harmed. Don't feel harmed — and you haven't been."
src: https://dailystoic.com/theres-a-reason-people-like-this-line/ (explicitly Hays) ; corroborated-by: https://www.michaeldmcgill.com/notes/meditations-marcus-aurelius-gregory-hays-translation/ ; confidence: high
note: Hays uses en/em dashes here; render with the site's dash convention but flag that the original punctuation uses dashes.

### 9.6
"Objective judgment, now, at this very moment. Unselfish action, now, at this very moment. Willing acceptance — now, at this very moment — of all external events. That's all you need."
src: https://www.goodreads.com/quotes/5429509 ; corroborated-by: https://jimbouman.com/meditations-marcus-aurelius-gregory-hays/ ; confidence: high

## Conflicts found during verification (do NOT trust without a real page)

These famous lines returned DIFFERENT "Hays" wordings across sources, so they
are NOT recorded as verified. They illustrate why a fragment-harvest of this
translation would be unreliable as well as a copyright concern:

- **5.16** — "the quality of your thoughts" (one source) vs "the colour of your
  thoughts" (another). Unresolved.
- **7.59** — "Look within. Within is the fountain of good..." vs "Dig deep
  within yourself, for there is a fountain of goodness..." attributed to Hays
  by different pages. Unresolved (the second looks like a different/loose
  translation).
- **10.16** — appeared as "Stop talking about what a good man is like, and just
  be one." AND as the popular "Waste no more time arguing what a good man should
  be. Be one." AND as unrelated text. The "Waste no more time arguing..." form
  is widely shared but was NOT confirmed to be Hays by any source that names the
  translator. Unresolved.
