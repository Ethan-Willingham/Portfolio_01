# The Chemical Path, research dossier

Engine D, the practice mapped: the entheogen route to the mystical. Sources verified
via web research 2026-06-20. Every quote checked verbatim where marked VERBATIM.
Contested claims flagged so the post does not overclaim.

## Spine
The oldest and most direct route to the mystical experience is chemical, and science
is rediscovering it. Map the path: the traditions, the classic account (Huxley), the
modern evidence (Pollan + the trials), the experience, and the honest risks.

## The frame quotes (VERBATIM, verified)
- William James, The Varieties of Religious Experience (1902), on nitrous oxide:
  "our normal waking consciousness, rational consciousness as we call it, is but one
  special type of consciousness, whilst all about it, parted from it by the filmiest
  of screens, there lie potential forms of consciousness entirely different."
- Blake, The Marriage of Heaven and Hell (1790): "If the doors of perception were
  cleansed every thing would appear to man as it is, Infinite." (Huxley's title; The
  Doors took their band name from the book.)
- Cicero, De Legibus, on the Eleusinian Mysteries: "we have learned from them the
  beginnings of life, and have gained the power not only to live happily, but also to
  die with a better hope."

## 1. The traditions
### Eleusinian Mysteries
- Ran ~2 millennia; ended 392 CE (Theodosius I ban on pagan rites) / Telesterion
  destroyed 396 CE by Alaric's Goths. Initiates incl. Plato, Cicero, Roman emperors.
  Secret kykeon drink; death penalty for revealing the rite.
- ERGOT HYPOTHESIS (CONTESTED): "The Road to Eleusis" (1978), R. Gordon Wasson +
  Albert Hofmann + Carl Ruck argued kykeon contained ergot (Claviceps) alkaloids, a
  natural lysergic-acid (LSD-like) source. FLAG: most classicists do not accept it;
  experimental confirmation that toxic ergopeptides convert to safe psychoactive LSA
  in an ancient prep is lacking. Present as a tantalizing, unproven hypothesis.

### Peyote + Native American Church
- Lophophora williamsii, small spineless cactus; active alkaloid mescaline. Native to
  n. Mexico + s. Texas (Chihuahuan Desert). Archaeological ceremonial use >= 5,700
  years (Rio Grande Valley specimens, radiocarbon).
- Quanah Parker (Comanche) leading advocate; NAC incorporated 1918. Half-moon
  ceremony: tipi, crescent earthen altar opening east, all-night fire, song, prayer.
- LAW: Employment Division v. Smith, 494 U.S. 872 (1990): no constitutional exemption
  for religious peyote use. American Indian Religious Freedom Act Amendments 1994
  (Pub. L. 103-344, Oct 6 1994): federal statutory protection for NAC peyote use.
- CONSERVATION CRISIS: wild peyote declining (IUCN >=30% over 20 yrs); slow-growing;
  Texas the only US habitat; membership grew (~300k to ~650k) while supply falls.

### Ayahuasca
- B. caapi vine (harmala alkaloids harmine/harmaline = MAO-A inhibitors) + P. viridis
  leaf (DMT). DMT is orally inactive (gut MAO destroys it); the vine's MAOI lets it
  reach the brain. Two-plant combination among thousands = remarkable indigenous
  pharmacology.
- Santo Daime: Raimundo Irineu Serra (Mestre Irineu), first trabalho May 26 1930.
  UDV (Centro Espirita Beneficente Uniao do Vegetal): founded 1961, Jose Gabriel da
  Costa. Gonzales v. O Centro UDV, 546 U.S. 418 (2006): UNANIMOUS, allowed sacramental
  ayahuasca under RFRA (Roberts opinion).
- Tourism risk: deaths in remote unregulated retreats (e.g. 2018 killing of Shipibo
  elder Olivia Arevalo; US Embassy Lima health alerts). Real danger, name it.

### Psilocybin + the Mazatec
- Wasson, "Seeking the Magic Mushroom," LIFE, May 13 1957: his 1955 velada with the
  Mazatec curandera Maria Sabina in Huautla de Jimenez, Oaxaca. Aztec teonanacatl,
  "flesh of the gods." Hofmann isolated/synthesized psilocybin 1958.
- Aftermath for Maria Sabina: blamed for revealing the rite, ostracized, house burned,
  son murdered, died in poverty. She said the mushrooms lost their purity once the
  outsiders came. The cost of the West "discovering" a living tradition.

### Word
- "Entheogen" coined 1979 (Ruck, Bigwood, Staples, Schultes, Ott, Wasson): "the divine
  within," a replacement for "hallucinogen" (implies false) and "psychedelic" (loaded).

## 2. The classic account: Huxley, The Doors of Perception (1954)
- Experiment: one morning May 1953, his LA home, 4/10 gram (400 mg) mescaline in water,
  supervised by psychiatrist Humphry Osmond (who was studying mescaline as a model of
  schizophrenia). Huxley expected visions; mostly got the transfiguration of ordinary
  things (the flowers, the chair, his trouser folds).
- REDUCING VALVE (VERBATIM): "To make biological survival possible, Mind at Large has
  to be funneled through the reducing valve of the brain and nervous system. The
  function of the brain and nervous system is to protect us from being overwhelmed and
  confused by this mass of largely useless and irrelevant knowledge, by shutting out
  most of what we should otherwise perceive or remember at any moment, and leaving only
  that very small and special selection which is likely to be practically useful." +
  "What comes out at the other end is a measly trickle of the kind of consciousness
  which will help us to stay alive." The drug widens the valve.
- Flowers (VERBATIM): "I was seeing what Adam had seen on the morning of his creation,
  the miracle, moment by moment, of naked existence." Istigkeit / "Is-ness" (Meister
  Eckhart's word).
- The word PSYCHEDELIC: Osmond coined it (announced 1957, NY Academy of Sciences),
  "mind-manifesting." Couplet exchange (VERBATIM): Huxley, "To make this trivial world
  sublime, / Take half a gram of phanerothyme." Osmond, "To fathom Hell or soar
  angelic, / Just take a pinch of psychedelic."
- Heaven and Hell (1956): the same door opens onto the infernal as well as the
  celestial; not all bliss. Honest caveat.
- Death: Nov 22 1963 (same day as JFK); dying of cancer, asked his wife Laura in
  writing for LSD (100 ug IM); she administered it.
- CRITIQUE: R.C. Zaehner, Mysticism Sacred and Profane (1957): a drug-induced state is
  not the same as theistic/sacred mystical attainment. Keep this honest tension in.

## 3. The modern evidence (Pollan + the trials)
- Pollan, How to Change Your Mind (2018): the "psychedelic renaissance," psychedelics
  as a tool for studying consciousness; respectful but not credulous.
- DEFAULT MODE NETWORK + EGO DISSOLUTION: Carhart-Harris (Imperial). Psilocybin
  decreases DMN activity/connectivity (the self-referential network); degree of DMN
  disintegration tracks subjective "ego dissolution." Papers: PNAS 2012 (neural
  correlates of the psychedelic state); "The entropic brain," Frontiers Hum Neurosci
  2014; Lebedev et al., "Finding the self by losing the self," Hum Brain Mapp 2015.
  The network that maintains the self goes quiet -> the mystic's union, in fMRI.
- MYSTICAL EXPERIENCE OPERATIONALIZED: W.T. Stace's criteria (unity, transcendence of
  time/space, noetic quality, sacredness, deeply-felt positive mood, ineffability,
  paradoxicality) -> Pahnke then Griffiths built the Mystical Experience Questionnaire
  (MEQ30). Repeated finding: the MEQ score (the intensity of the mystical experience)
  predicts the clinical benefit. The mystical experience is the active ingredient, not
  just a side effect.

### Trials (exact)
- Griffiths et al. 2006, Psychopharmacology 187(3):268-283. 36 healthy volunteers.
  ~two-thirds rated the psilocybin session among the five most personally meaningful
  and spiritually significant experiences of their lives (about a third, the single
  most), comparable to the birth of a first child; sustained at 14 months.
- Good Friday / Marsh Chapel, Pahnke 1962 (Apr 20), under Leary/Alpert (Harvard
  Psilocybin Project). 20 divinity students, double-blind psilocybin vs niacin. Most on
  psilocybin reported genuine mystical experiences. DARK DETAIL: one fled the chapel
  and was restrained and injected with thorazine. Doblin 1991 follow-up (J Transpersonal
  Psychol): located 19/20; psilocybin group still called it a spiritual high point, but
  he flagged the protocol's flaws (weak placebo, observer bias).
- Cancer end-of-life trials, 2016, J Psychopharmacology. Griffiths (Johns Hopkins,
  n=51) and Ross (NYU, n=29). A single high dose cut depression and anxiety; ~60-80%
  with clinically significant, sustained (~6 month) improvement. Benefit tracked the
  intensity of the mystical experience.
- Carhart-Harris et al. 2021, NEJM, psilocybin vs escitalopram (SSRI), n=59. PRIMARY
  outcome (QIDS-SR-16 change at 6 wk) NOT statistically significant between groups
  (-8.0 vs -6.0). Secondary measures (response 70% vs 48%, remission 57% vs 28%)
  favored psilocybin but did not establish superiority. Honest: a draw on the primary.
- COMPASS COMP360, Goodwin et al. 2022, NEJM, n=233 treatment-resistant depression.
  25mg vs 10mg vs 1mg. Week 3: response 37%, remission 29%; sustained response at wk 12
  only ~20%. SAFETY: 3 of the 25mg patients showed suicidal behavior (none in the lower
  doses), mostly nonresponders, weeks after dosing. Do not bury this.
- Smoking, Johnson/Garcia-Romeu/Griffiths 2014, J Psychopharmacology. 12 of 15 (80%)
  abstinent at 6 months. TINY pilot (n=15). Striking but unproven at scale.

## 4. Overhype / truth over narrative (REQUIRED, do not cheerlead)
- Small samples, enthusiast volunteers.
- BROKEN BLINDING ("functional unblinding"): subjects and therapists almost always know
  who got the drug, which inflates effects via expectancy. The central methodological
  problem of the whole field.
- The therapy confound: it is drug + intensive psychotherapy, hard to separate.
- For-profit gold rush, conflicts of interest.
- MICRODOSING is mostly placebo: Szigeti et al. 2021, eLife, "self-blinding citizen
  science." Microdosers improved, but so did placebo; no significant between-group
  difference. The benefits are expectation.
- THE MDMA REALITY CHECK (the strongest cautionary fact): MAPS/Lykos Phase 3 for PTSD.
  MAPP1 (Mitchell 2021, Nature Medicine): 67% of MDMA arm no longer met PTSD criteria
  vs 32% placebo. MAPP2 (2023): 71% vs 48%. STRONG numbers. Yet: FDA advisory committee
  June 4 2024 voted 2-9 against effectiveness and 1-10 that benefits outweigh risks;
  FDA issued a Complete Response Letter (rejection) to Lykos Aug 2024; the journal
  Psychopharmacology retracted 3 MDMA papers for unethical conduct (a documented case of
  a participant inappropriately touched by therapists in a Phase 2 session, on video).
  Real science, real integrity problems. The hype ran ahead of the proof.

## 5. The risks (accurate, neither scare nor minimize)
- Physiological toxicity of classic serotonergic psychedelics (psilocybin, LSD,
  mescaline, DMT) is LOW; fatal overdose from the drug itself is essentially unknown.
  The danger is psychological and behavioral, not the LD50.
- Bad trips: acute terror, panic, paranoia; dangerous behavior (accidents, falls).
- PSYCHOSIS: can trigger prolonged psychosis or unmask latent schizophrenia/bipolar,
  esp. with personal/family history. THE key contraindication.
- HPPD (persisting perception disorder): lingering visual disturbances; rare, real.
- Cardiac/interactions: ayahuasca's MAOI + SSRIs/SNRIs or tyramine foods = serotonin
  syndrome / hypertensive risk. MDMA: hyperthermia, cardiac, hyponatremia.
- SET AND SETTING (Leary's phrase, from Osmond/Hubbard): mindset + environment decide
  heaven vs hell. The screened patient with a guide in a clinic is in a different world
  from the unprepared recreational user. The single most important safety idea.
- THE LEARY CAUTIONARY TALE: Leary + Alpert (Ram Dass), Harvard Psilocybin Project,
  fired 1963; "turn on, tune in, drop out"; the counterculture backlash put psychedelics
  in Schedule I (CSA 1970) and froze legitimate research for ~a generation. The hype
  destroyed the science once already. That is the post's warning, made concrete.

## 6. The law now (precise)
- Federal: psilocybin, LSD, mescaline, DMT, MDMA, peyote all Schedule I (illegal).
- Religious exemptions: peyote for NAC members (AIRFA 1994); ayahuasca for UDV
  (Gonzales 2006) and Santo Daime, under RFRA.
- Oregon Measure 109 (2020): first regulated psilocybin SERVICES (licensed centers,
  supervised adult use, opened 2023). NOT a prescription/medical model.
- Colorado Prop 122 (2022, Natural Medicine Health Act): decriminalized + regulated
  access for psilocybin, psilocin, DMT, ibogaine, mescaline (not peyote).
- Denver (May 2019): first US city to decriminalize psilocybin. "Legal," "decriminalized,"
  and "regulated access" are three different things; keep them distinct.
- No classic psychedelic is FDA-approved as of mid-2025; psilocybin holds Breakthrough
  Therapy designations (COMPASS, Usona).
