# OPTIONAL_BODY_BIBLE.md — Content Bible

The canonical **content** reference for the interactive blog post **"How Much of You Do You Need?"** (working title; alternates: *Spare Parts*, *The Optional Body*, *Subtract Yourself*). Project codename: **Optional Body**.

This doc owns the **what** — the concept, the arc, the tone, and every vetted fact with its source. The **how** (art direction, scrollytelling craft, technical architecture) lives in [`OPTIONAL_BODY_STYLE.md`](OPTIONAL_BODY_STYLE.md). Read both before building.

> **Status:** research round 1 complete. Facts below are sourced, but some headline numbers need a stronger citation before publish — see §9 *Sourcing to-do*. This is a living doc; flip items and add session logs as we go.

---

## 1. The concept, in one sentence

A scroll-driven descent where the reader takes a human body apart piece by piece — and keeps discovering it's *fine* — starting with parts you didn't know you had, sinking through organs you'd miss but survive, past limbs, down to a torso wired to machines, and finally into the brain itself, until the question stops being *"what can you remove?"* and becomes *"where did the **you** go?"*

The whole piece is a **subtraction game**. The reader removes, the body keeps living, and a single quiet status line — **`Still alive ✓`** — refuses to flinch. Until, at the very bottom, it does.

---

## 2. The core conceit & THE TURN (plan the ending first)

The format research is unanimous: the best neal.fun pieces are built backwards from a final **turn** that recontextualizes the whole scroll (Deep Sea: you hit the bottom; Size of Space: the human vanishes; Spend Bill Gates' Money: you *can't* spend it). Ours:

- **The whole descent (Tiers 0–4):** removing things *keeps you alive*. Every subtraction is survivable. The reader's growing feeling: *the body is almost entirely optional.*
- **The turn (Tier 5→6):** in the brain, the rule inverts. We show the body surviving astonishing **structural** brain loss (half a brain gone, a skull mostly fluid) — and then the gut-punch: a body in a persistent vegetative state can breathe, sleep, open its eyes for *years* with, as far as we can tell, **no one home.** Remove the right thing and the body lives but the **person** is gone.
- **The landing:** so *you* were never the parts. You're the pattern — the one item on the entire list nobody can locate on a scan, cleanly remove, or even agree how to define. The body is almost entirely optional. **The self is the one non-negotiable, and we don't know what it's made of.** The `Still alive ✓` finally becomes **`Still you?`** — no checkmark. The last thing on screen is the reader.

**Structural through-line (use this, it's the spine):** every machine in Tier 4 replaces a *mechanical or filtering* job — a pump (heart), a bellows (lungs), a sieve (kidneys). The two things we **cannot** replace — the **liver** and the **brain** — are the organs that *create and decide* rather than move things. The descent is really the slow reveal that "you" is not plumbing.

## 3. Tone & sensitivity guardrails (read before writing a single line of copy)

These are real surgeries on real people, and the deep tiers touch amputation, organ donation, brain injury, disorders of consciousness, and death. Get the tone wrong and the piece is ghoulish instead of awed.

- **Curious and a little awed, never mocking.** The "funny" is cosmic-absurd ("the body is a clown car of optional parts"), never cruel about a specific condition or person.
- **Real people, named with respect.** Paul Alexander (70+ years in an iron lung), Craig Lewis (first pulseless artificial heart), quadruple-amputee thrivers — these are humans, not exhibits.
- **Survival ≠ unchanged.** Never say "perfectly fine." People living without these parts have real deficits and real maintenance. The honest framing — *you can live without this, here's the cost* — is **more** interesting than a fake "no downside."
- **Bust the myths, don't repeat them.** The single biggest credibility risk is parroting pop-science. See §8 for the specific ones (appendix "useless," "90% of his brain," "two consciousnesses," "no heartbeat").
- **Humility at the consciousness tiers.** PVS↔minimally-conscious misdiagnosis runs ~37–43%; some "vegetative" patients show covert awareness on fMRI. Never state "no one home" as certain — state it as *what we can detect.*
- **A light footer note:** "These are real procedures performed on real people. This is a curiosity piece, not medical advice."

## 4. The running counter — the narrative spine

Every great piece has **one persistent number/state** the reader watches change (depth, balance, scale). Ours is a small, always-visible HUD with up to three readouts:

- **`Still alive ✓`** — the spine. Holds green through the entire descent. This is the joke and the dread: it *never flinches*, no matter what we remove — until consciousness, where it becomes **`Still you?`** with no checkmark.
- **`Removed: N`** — a running tally of parts subtracted. Climbs into the dozens.
- **`Body remaining: ~X%`** — optional, by mass/volume. Drops toward a sliver. (Numbers are illustrative, not clinical — keep them honest-ish, label them as rough.)
- **The "asterisk economy" (see §7):** as the descent deepens, survival picks up a visible *cost* — a pill icon, then a machine icon, then a donor icon. The counter quietly shows that staying alive stops being free.

## 5. The descent — tiers & stations

Each **station** = one body part = a short scene + one verified fact. Field format per station: **What it is · Removal/absence · Survivability (+cost) · The HOOK · Source(s) · Confidence/caveat.** Facts are research-round-1 verified; caveats are load-bearing.

### TIER 0 — The frame
**Spine ligament (the personal hook).** Open on the true story: a microdiscectomy removes a piece of the spine — a ligament (ligamentum flavum is partially removed in the procedure) — and the surgeon says you'll never miss it. He's right. *That's the rule of the game: we only remove what you can live without.* Start the counter at `Removed: 1 · Still alive ✓`.
> *Note: confirm the exact structure removed in a standard microdiscectomy (ligamentum flavum / part of the lamina) before finalizing copy — this is the author's own surgery, so it can be told first-person and verified personally.*

### TIER 1 — "You won't even notice" (spare parts & true leftovers)
The light tier. Several are **do-it-yourself body checks** the reader can perform right now — the strongest interaction type for this tier.

- **Palmaris longus** (★ self-check) — *What:* a thin wrist-flexor tendon. *Absence:* ~**10–15%** of people are simply born without it (varies hugely by ethnicity, ~4% to >25%); surgeons harvest it for tendon grafts because it's expendable. *Survivability:* zero effect on grip/pinch. *HOOK + interaction:* **touch thumb to pinky and flex your wrist — does a cord pop up?** ~1 in 7 people see nothing. *Source:* [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palmaris_longus_muscle), [PMC prevalence](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10708979/), [PMC grip/pinch](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3652998/). *Caveat:* "15%" is a textbook average — quote a range, it's genuinely ethnicity-dependent.
- **Appendix** — *What:* a finger-sized blind pouch off the large intestine. *Removal:* appendectomy, one of the most common emergency surgeries; lifetime appendicitis risk ~**5–9%**. *HOOK (reversal):* the "useless organ" cliché is **wrong** — the leading hypothesis (Duke, 2007) is it's a **"safe house"** that re-seeds your gut bacteria after illness, and it's evolved independently 30+ times in mammals. *Source:* [NIDDK](https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/digestive-diseases/appendicitis/definition-facts), [Duke Health](https://corporate.dukehealth.org/news/appendix-isnt-useless-all-its-safe-house-bacteria). *Caveat:* frame as "maybe not useless," not "is essential" — it's a well-supported hypothesis, not settled fact.
- **Wisdom teeth** — ~**22%** of people globally are born missing at least one (range ~5–40%, highest in East Asians). *HOOK:* evolution mid-act — shrinking jaws are dropping the back molars out of the gene pool (heritability 60–80%). *Source:* [PMC agenesis](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7678444/).
- **Accessory navicular bone** — an extra foot bone present in ~**10–12%** of people; only ~0.1% ever have trouble with it. *HOOK:* 1 in 10 people walk on a bonus bone and never know. *Source:* [PMC meta-analysis](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11405447/).
- **Coccyx / tailbone** — 3–5 fused vertebrae, the literal remnant of a tail; removable (coccygectomy). *HOOK:* you grew an actual tail (~10–12 vertebrae, week 5–6) and reabsorbed it by week 8. *Source:* [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coccyx). *Caveat:* not functionless — it anchors pelvic-floor muscles and bears weight when sitting; "useless" overstates it.
- **Auricular (ear) muscles** — vestigial ear-swivel muscles; only ~10–20% can wiggle their ears. *HOOK:* a **2025 study** found these "dead" muscles still fire electrically when you strain to listen — your brain still tries to point ears that no longer move. *Source:* [Frontiers 2025](https://www.frontiersin.org/news/2025/01/31/ear-muscle-wiggling-ears-activates-listening-frontiers-neuroscience).
- **Darwin's tubercle** — a small point on the ear's rim, present on a sizable fraction of ears. *HOOK:* named by **Darwin himself** in *The Descent of Man* as the leftover tip of a folded-over pointed ancestral ear. *Source:* [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Darwin%27s_tubercle). *Caveat:* prevalence figures are wildly inconsistent; it is **not** a clean dominant gene (old textbook myth).
- **Plica semilunaris** (the "third eyelid") — the pink crescent in your eye's inner corner. *HOOK:* it's the vestige of the **nictitating membrane** — the milky third eyelid you can watch flick across a bird's or cat's eye. Yours can't. *Source:* [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plica_semilunaris_of_conjunctiva). *Caveat:* aids tear drainage — "vestigial as a third eyelid," not idle.
- **Arrector pili / goosebumps** — tiny muscles that yank each hair upright. *HOOK:* goosebumps are you trying to **fluff a fur coat you no longer have.** *Source:* [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arrector_pili_muscle). *Nuance:* the same muscle-nerve unit helps regulate hair-follicle stem cells (Hsu lab, *Cell* 2020) — not entirely pointless.
- **Tonsils & adenoids** — immune tissue at the throat/nasal back; >**500,000** tonsillectomies/yr in U.S. kids under 15. *HOOK (reversal):* yanked out reflexively for decades, then doctors realized they're **functioning immune tissue** — the surgery is far less casual now. *Source:* [NCBI StatPearls](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK536942/). *Caveat:* the least "zero-consequence" item here — frame as "removable and survivable," not "useless."

> **Tier-1 framing tip:** split honestly into *true leftovers* (palmaris, wisdom teeth, navicular, coccyx, ear muscles, tubercle, plica, arrector pili) vs. *"surprise — not useless after all"* (appendix, tonsils). The reversal ("science called it junk, then found its job") is a **better hook** than "useless."

### TIER 2 — "Okay, that's serious. You're still fine." (organs)
Real organs, real surgery, demonstrably full lives. **Suggested order = free → costly** (a difficulty ramp that ends each beat harder): Gallbladder → One kidney → Breasts → Spleen → One lung → **Liver (showstopper)** → Colon → Uterus → Stomach → **Thyroid (gut-punch)**. Cost tags: **(A)** free · **(B)** daily pill · **(C)** machine · **(D)** donor.

- **Gallbladder** **(A)** — cholecystectomy, one of the most common abdominal ops. *HOOK:* nothing replaces it — the liver just switches from batch-delivering bile to a permanent slow drip, and you don't notice. *Source:* [NIH StatPearls](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK448145/). *Caveat:* annual-volume figures disagree (300k vs 600k–1.2M) — say "hundreds of thousands a year," not a point number.
- **One kidney** **(A)** — living donors show no drop in life expectancy. *HOOK:* ~**1 in 750–1,000** people have only one kidney (renal agenesis) and most find out by accident on an unrelated scan. *Source:* [NIDDK](https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/kidney-disease/solitary-kidney), [UCLA Health](https://www.uclahealth.org/news/article/donating-kidney-does-not-affect-life-expectancy). *Caveat:* ~1 in 2,000 *births* vs ~1 in 750–1,000 *living* — cite the range.
- **Breasts / nipples** **(A)** — mastectomy; not a survival organ. *HOOK:* surgeons can remove **all** internal breast tissue through a hidden incision while keeping skin and nipple (nipple-sparing); immediate reconstruction went from ~10% (1980s) to ~90% today. *Source:* [Cleveland Clinic](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/procedures/nipple-sparing-mastectomy).
- **Spleen** **(B)** — splenectomy; liver and lymph nodes absorb most of its jobs. *HOOK:* the lifelong threat (OPSI sepsis) is rare (~5% lifetime) but terrifyingly fast — a "normal" fever can become **death within ~12–24 hours**, so asplenic people treat any fever as an emergency. Requires lifelong vaccines ± standby antibiotics. *Source:* [Cleveland Clinic](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/procedures/14614-splenectomy), [OPSI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overwhelming_post-splenectomy_infection).
- **One lung** **(A)** — pneumonectomy; the other lung expands to fill the space. *HOOK:* people don't just live with one lung, they **exercise** with it — capacity ~halves, daily life is normal. *Source:* [Cleveland Clinic](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/treatments/25003-pneumonectomy).
- **Liver — THE SHOWSTOPPER** **(A)**, full removal **(D)** — partial hepatectomy / living donation takes 25–65%. *HOOK:* the **only internal organ that regrows** — as little as ~10–25% can regenerate to full size in ~8–12 weeks; a living donor gives away most of a lobe and both halves grow back. *Source:* [UPMC](https://www.upmc.com/services/transplant/services/procedures/living-liver-donor-surgery), [PMC regeneration](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7665117/). **This is the emotional peak of Tier 2 — give it room.**
- **Colon / large intestine** **(A, rerouted)** — total colectomy; surgeons build a **new "rectum" out of your small intestine** (the J-pouch) so you go normally, no bag. *HOOK:* the J-pouch. *Source:* [Cleveland Clinic](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/treatments/21062-j-pouch-surgery).
- **Uterus / reproductive organs** **(A, B if ovaries too)** — hysterectomy, ~500–600k/yr in the US. *HOOK:* by their 70s, **nearly 1 in 2** US women have had one (~21% overall; ~44% at 70–74). Keep the ovaries and hormones continue as normal. *Source:* [PMC trends](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9529796/), [CDC NCHS](https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/data/series/sr_13/sr13_092.pdf). *Caveat:* the popular "1 in 3 by 60" is age-band-dependent — use verified figures.
- **Stomach** **(B)** — total gastrectomy joins esophagus straight to small intestine. *HOOK:* with no holding tank, food dumps into the intestine — **"dumping syndrome"** — so you eat many tiny meals all day; plus lifelong B12 (the stomach made the factor needed to absorb it). *Source:* [NCI](https://www.cancer.gov/news-events/cancer-currents-blog/2024/preventive-gastrectomy-long-term-consequences).
- **Thyroid — THE GUT-PUNCH** **(B, non-negotiable)** — thyroidectomy. *HOOK:* the **first organ on the whole list you cannot live without maintenance** — survivable *only* with a daily levothyroxine pill, forever; stop it and severe hypothyroidism can be fatal. But the "machine" replacing an entire gland is one tiny pill each morning. *Source:* [American Thyroid Association](https://www.thyroid.org/thyroid-hormone-treatment/). **End Tier 2 here — it's the cliff into the machine tiers.**

### TIER 3 — "Now we're getting weird" (limbs & external structures)
Heavy visuals, dark-funny. The figure on screen is now down to a torso + head.

- **Quadruple amputation** — people live full, active lives with no arms and no legs (torso, organs, brain untouched). *HOOK:* Curley Christian, buried alive at Vimy Ridge in 1917, became the Commonwealth's only known surviving WWI quadruple amputee and supported his family selling cigars; modern survivors run businesses and train for marathons. *Source:* [Legion Magazine](https://legionmagazine.com/quadruple-amputee-inspired-others/), [TIME](https://time.com/4103487/quadruple-amputee-veteran-todd-nicely/). *Caveat:* these are notable thrivers, not a typical baseline.
- **External face structures** — nose (rhinectomy), ears (auriculectomy), eyes (bilateral enucleation → blindness), jaw (mandibulectomy), each individually survivable, usually for cancer. *HOOK:* the **outer ear is acoustically almost optional** (a passive funnel — removing it doesn't by itself cause deafness), and an **entire jaw can be rebuilt from a leg bone** (fibula free flap). *Source:* [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/medical-professionals/otolaryngology/news/restoring-hearing-after-ear-removal-in-patients-with-skin-cancer/mac-20591149), [OncoLink](https://www.oncolink.org/cancers/head-and-neck/treatments/surgical-treatments/mandibulectomy). *Caveat:* jaw removal usually means reconstruction, not living jawless long-term.

### TIER 4 — "The bare minimum" (the machine-supported core)
The figure is now a core wired to a wall of machines. **The mechanical/filtering jobs can all be outsourced:**

- **Heart → continuous-flow LVAD / total artificial heart.** ★ **THE signature beat.** *HOOK (verified hard):* a conscious, talking patient on a continuous-flow device can have **no detectable pulse at all** — the peer-reviewed AHA journal literally titled its review **"Living Without a Pulse."** Craig Lewis (2011, Texas Heart Institute) got a *total* continuous-flow heart — his native heart removed — and doctors heard not a heartbeat but **a steady whir like a boat propeller.** *Source:* [Circ Heart Fail "Living Without a Pulse"](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29903893/), [Texas Monthly](https://www.texasmonthly.com/the-culture/houstons-pulseless-artificial-heart-revisited/). ⚠️ **Accuracy:** say "no *pulse* / non-pulsatile flow" for LVAD patients (the native heart still has electrical activity — *not* a flatline EKG); reserve "no heartbeat" for Craig Lewis, whose heart was actually removed. Don't conflate them. **Interaction:** "feel for a pulse" — a dot beats, then flatlines, but `Still alive ✓` holds.
- **Lungs → ECMO / ventilator.** *HOOK:* gas exchange happens **entirely outside the body**; people have lived **200+ days** on ECMO awaiting a lung transplant (a 207-day case; a 403-day case reported). And Paul Alexander lived **70+ years** in an iron lung — earning a law degree and practicing as an attorney from inside it. *Source:* [Johns Hopkins ECMO](https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/health/treatment-tests-and-therapies/extracorporeal-membrane-oxygenation), [Guinness — iron lung](https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/news/2023/3/meet-longest-surviving-iron-lung-patient-kept-alive-by-a-machine-for-70-years-741881).
- **Kidneys → dialysis.** *HOOK:* "average" dialysis survival is quoted at 5–10 years, yet record-holders have lived **~43–47 years** on it. *Source:* [Guinness — dialysis](https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/world-records/longest-time-having-kidney-dialysis-(haemodialysis)).
- **The wall — what CANNOT be replaced.** *HOOK:* there is **no machine that replaces the liver** (its toxins are protein-bound; "artificial liver" devices only bridge temporarily) — and none for the brain. **This is the structural through-line landing:** machines replace pumps, bellows, and sieves. The liver and the brain *create and decide.* That's the doorway into the final tier.

### TIER 5 — "What is the *you* that's left?" (the brain)
The body tolerates astonishing **structural** brain loss:

- **Hemispherectomy** — half the cerebrum removed/disconnected (usually children, for catastrophic epilepsy). *HOOK:* in the Johns Hopkins series of 71 children, IQ changed <15 points in 34 of 53, **8 improved**, and 65% became seizure-free — language and personality often intact thanks to childhood neuroplasticity. *Source:* [Pulsifer et al., *Epilepsia* 2004](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15009226/). *Caveat:* outcomes depend on cause and age — true *only* for early surgery on an already-damaged hemisphere; in adults the deficits are far worse.
- **The Feuillet 2007 *Lancet* case** — a 44-year-old French civil servant whose skull was largely filled with cerebrospinal fluid (chronic hydrocephalus), brain compressed to a thin rim — married, two kids, employed, IQ ~75. *Source:* [*Lancet* 370:262](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0140-6736(07)61127-1). ⚠️ **Accuracy — bust the myth loudly:** the viral "**90% of his brain missing**" is misleading. The tissue was **compressed, not absent**; "90%" is an eyeballed ventricle estimate; and he was **not** unimpaired (IQ 75). Honest version: "most of his cranial volume was fluid, his brain squeezed thin — and he lived an ordinary life anyway."
- **Split-brain** — corpus callosotomy severs the ~200M-fiber bridge between hemispheres; each disconnected half can respond to things the other denies seeing (Sperry won the 1981 Nobel). *HOOK:* a patient's left hand could pick out an object his speaking self insisted he never saw. *Source:* [PMC review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7305066/). ⚠️ **Accuracy:** the strong "**two consciousnesses**" reading is **no longer consensus** — Pinto et al. (2017) found unified response control even when perception splits. Present as an **open mystery**, not "two minds." The honest verdict: *"the evidence is insufficient to answer this question."*
- **Cerebellar agenesis** — born essentially without a cerebellum (which holds ~80% of the brain's neurons). *HOOK:* a woman reached adulthood — walking unsteadily, speaking, married — before a scan revealed her entire cerebellum was simply *missing*; only ~a dozen living cases are documented. *Source:* [*Brain* 2015](https://academic.oup.com/brain/article/138/6/e353/269537). *Caveat:* real motor/cognitive deficits — astonishing survival + compensation, not "unaffected."

### TIER 6 — The turn & the ending (its own mini-project)
- **The literal turn — brain death vs PVS vs MCS.** Define them accurately: **brain death** = irreversible loss of *all* brain function incl. brainstem (= legally/medically dead, machines only); **persistent vegetative state** = wakefulness *without awareness* (brainstem intact: sleep-wake cycles, breathing, eye-opening for years); **minimally conscious state** = intermittent, reproducible awareness. *HOOK:* the arc made literal — in PVS a body can breathe, sleep, wake, open its eyes and grimace **for years** with no one detectably home. *Source:* [Merck Manual](https://www.merckmanuals.com/professional/neurologic-disorders/coma-and-impaired-consciousness/vegetative-state-and-minimally-conscious-state). ⚠️ **Sensitivity:** never use brain-dead / vegetative / coma interchangeably; PVS↔MCS misdiagnosis ~37–43%, so "no one home" must stay humble.
- **The landing — where the knife can't reach.** We mapped, cut, drained and halved the body and the brain, and the one thing the journey was *about* — the felt experience of being *you* — is the one thing no scan can point to. The **hard problem of consciousness** (Chalmers): why is any of this *felt* at all? **IIT** (Tononi) is a leading but contested hypothesis (publicly challenged in 2023 — flag it, don't present as proven). The **brain-in-a-vat** even unmoors "the body" you spent the whole piece dismantling. *Source:* [IEP hard problem](https://iep.utm.edu/hard-problem-of-conciousness/), [SEP neuroscience of consciousness](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/consciousness-neuroscience/). **Honest bottom line (this IS the ending, not a cop-out):** consciousness currently *cannot be located, cleanly removed, or even agreed-upon as defined.*
- **Final beat:** `Still alive ✓` → `Still you?` (no checkmark). The figure dissolves into the reader / the screen / the cursor. One last line, then silence.

## 7. Accuracy guardrails (the myth-busting checklist — never ship without this)

1. **Appendix & tonsils are NOT "useless."** The real (better) story is the reversal: science called them junk, then found their jobs.
2. **Feuillet: NOT "90% of his brain missing."** Compressed, not absent; eyeballed estimate; IQ 75.
3. **Split-brain: NOT "two consciousnesses."** Contested; recent work favors more unity. An open question.
4. **No-*pulse* (LVAD) vs no-*heartbeat* (Craig Lewis's removed heart) are different claims.** Keep them distinct.
5. **Brain-dead ≠ vegetative ≠ coma.** Brain death *is* death. PVS↔MCS misdiagnosis ~37–43% → state awareness humbly.
6. **IIT is a contested hypothesis, not established science** (2023 open-letter controversy).
7. **Darwin's tubercle is not a simple dominant gene** (old textbook myth).
8. **Phrase as ranges, not points:** palmaris prevalence, gallbladder annual volume, single-kidney prevalence, hysterectomy prevalence, liver % that regrows.
9. **"Survivable," never "no downside."** Every removal carries real cost — say it.
