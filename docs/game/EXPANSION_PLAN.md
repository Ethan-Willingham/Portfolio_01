# EXPANSION_PLAN.md: Sluice: Towns, No Man's Zones, and Combat

This is the **living main plan** for Sluice's biggest expansion: turning the single centered town into a wide horizontal world of four progressively-deeper towns, separated by combat "No Man's Zone" flying gauntlets, with a new auto-turret / enemy / projectile combat system.

**How to use this doc.** It is the shared source of truth across sessions and across the parallel LLM accounts working this repo. Read it at the start of any expansion session. Revisit and update it at every phase boundary (see Revisit checkpoints). The Deviation log is append-only: when research or reality changes the plan, record it there rather than silently editing the past. Pair it with the project memory and with `AGENTS.md`.

Status: **P0 SHIPPED** (region scaffolding + the wide-world flip, v23.48). **P1 in progress**: stage 1 Layers (v23.53) and stage 2 Specials+Ores (v23.58, incl. ore veins) shipped; stage 3 Liquids in progress: architecture re-locked 2026-05-29 to EXTEND the WebGPU MLS-MPM particle sim (owner directive); the earlier two-tier cellular detour (v23.60-62) was reverted. Next: the behavior-preserving material-id refactor. Started 2026-05-28.

**COMBAT CORE SHIPPED 2026-05-30** (built ahead of P1 stage-3 liquids, on owner direction), all in the new `085-combat.js`: **P3** rig auto-turret + ground turrets + ownership bullet pool (v24.14); **P4-partial** flying chaser dash-drones with Reynolds steering + windup/lunge + hull-damage rams (v24.19); **P8-partial** trauma screenshake + hitstop + knockback (v24.21); intercept/lead aiming for all shooters (v24.22); **P5-partial** homing missiles (capped-turn-rate pursuit) from elite turrets (v24.24); **third-faction** feral **stinger swarm** (v24.33) — Boids flocking predators that attack the rig AND savage the turrets/drones (a three-way fight). REMAINING combat: P4 sniper + bruiser archetypes, P5 bomb-as-weapon, P6 turret shop upgrades, P7 zone difficulty scaling + threat banners, and a final balance pass. Tuning across all combat is a deliberately gentle first pass; owner playtest pending for feel.

## 1. Vision and scope

**In scope (this program):**
- A wide world: `ocean | TOWN 4 | NMZ3 | TOWN 3 | NMZ2 | TOWN 2 | NMZ1 | TOWN 1 (start) | ocean`.
- Four towns, each with its own surface (all aligned in altitude), its own layer stack, and its own ore table. Each town drills **deeper** than the last (Town 1 ~350, Town 4 ~1400).
- Three No Man's Zones: wide open-air combat gauntlets the player must FLY across. Underground is inaccessible (3 dirt rows then bomb-proof solid).
- Combat, only inside the No Man's Zones: player rig auto-turrets (auto-target, upgradable in shop) plus the existing droppable bombs, versus flying chasers, ground turrets, obstacles, and homing plus non-homing projectiles.
- Gear-gated progression: you must upgrade the rig to survive each crossing; each successive zone is harder.
- Decor for the new land: trees and ambient birds in the safe zones; pixel-art "No Man's Zone" banners at the safe-zone boundaries.

**Explicitly out of scope (deferred):**
- The trading system's physical multi-town markets (buy in one town, sell in another). The dynamic price MODEL is now BUILT and live as of 2026-05-30 (ECONOMY_BIBLE.md, js/sluice/265-economy.js): per-town specialization, drift, saturation, telegraphed shocks, cross-town quotes, all gm-tunable. The physical multi-town stores then landed too (v24.30-32): each of the 4 towns renders its own recoloured store and the Board opens in whichever town you stand in (it prices that town), so the spatial buy-here/sell-there loop is now playable. What still stays deferred, both optional: per-town refuel pumps + the ore auto-sell pad (Town-1 only today; the fireplace/pump/chimney stay home), and the No Man's Zone risk on carried trade goods.
- Any save/progress system (game stays session-only, per existing invariants).
- A win condition. The game stays endless (no win/credits), per the locked design.

## 2. Locked decisions and decision log

| D3 | Ocean on both far edges. One No Man's Zone = **90 seconds of flight at top speed with no obstacles** (so zones are very wide; true flight top speed must be measured, it is drag-limited below the 720 px/s hard cap). | 2026-05-28 |
| D4 | Zone underground is **virtual**: `tileAt` computes it (depth < 3 = shared frozen dirt, deeper = bomb-proof shared frozen `voidrock`), stored in no array. Decided up front (a refinement of D1) because 1400-deep plus 90s-wide zones would otherwise make a full flat array ~5.9M slots, mostly inert zone columns. | 2026-05-28 |

**Decision log (rationale, append as decisions change):**
- D1/D4: the only width-scaling costs are the `world` array and `generateWorld()` time. Rendering already scales with the viewport (terrain chunk cache is LRU-capped at 180). Camera, player clamp, and `tileAt` edges already key off `COLS * TILE`. So widening is a worldgen problem, and virtualizing the inert zones removes the bulk of the waste.
- D2: "deeper towns" means the deep bands below today's mantle (depth 400) are brand-new content (layers, ores, hazards). That is the bulk of the new mining content and ships per town.
- D1 (refined 2026-05-28 after P0 research): two independent research agents validated the region-table + virtual-zone + per-region-rules architecture as exactly what Terraria/Starbound do, and confirmed streaming is NOT warranted at this scale. They split on the raw memory estimate (~48 MB optimistic vs ~70 to 85 MB if hidden-class shapes drift); the conservative bound risks the uncatchable mobile-Safari page ceiling (~100 MB on an iPhone SE 3, with the GPU sims already consuming part of it). Resolution: ship the cheap, no-big-refactor mitigations (frozen inert prototypes + copy-on-write breakables, which keep the `world[r][c].type`/`.hp` API intact and collapse resident unique objects from ~1.1M to under ~100k), measure, escalate to SoA only if needed.

## 3. Architecture spine

**The one central change.** Today ores and layers are chosen by depth alone (`pickOre(depth)`, `getLayerForDepth(depth)`), with zero horizontal awareness, and the town is hardcoded at the array center. The backbone of the whole expansion is a left-to-right **region model**.

- **Region table + `colRegion[c]` lookup.** An ordered list of regions `{ kind: 'ocean'|'town'|'nomans', c0, c1, townIndex }`, plus a precomputed per-column lookup (O(1)), rebuilt only at worldgen.
- **Coordinate width vs stored width.** `WORLD_COLS` is the full coordinate width (towns + zones + oceans) used by the camera, player clamp, and `tileAt` bounds. The stored `world` array holds only town + ocean columns. Zone columns are virtual (D4).
- **Region-aware generation and lookup.** `pickOre(depth, townIndex)` and `getLayerForRegion(depth, townIndex)` index per-town tables. `generateWorld()` loops regions: towns run the existing pipeline (CA smoothing, void pockets, host-locking, barrier band, ponds, oil, jello, foundation) confined to their column slice; oceans get a water basin; zones store nothing.
- **Per-town deck / foundation / spawn.** Replace the single `DECK_CENTER_COL` with per-town deck centers. Spawn at Town 1.
- **Depth model.** All town surfaces at the same row. Each town has its own mineable floor depth and its own `LAYERS`-style stack and ore table. Below a town's floor, down to the global `TOTAL_ROWS`, is shared bedrock fill (free). `TOTAL_ROWS` is set by Town 4 (~1404).
- **Combat system** lives in a new fragment (provisionally `085-combat.js`, between update and render). It reuses existing primitives: `hitPauseT` (hitstop), `damageFlashT` (red flash), `explosions`, `liveBombs`, and `player.hull`.

**Storage and memory invariants (research-locked 2026-05-28).** Both P0 architecture agents converged on these; treat them as hard rules:
- **Everything reads through `tileAt`.** Its only world read is `return world[r][c]`, and `getTileObj`/`solidAt` funnel through it, so the region branch (and the virtual-zone computation) lives entirely inside `tileAt`. No caller touches `world[r][c]` directly, or the zone holes crash something. Audit the terrain chunk renderer and collision for direct reads.
- **Virtual zone tiles return SHARED FROZEN prototypes** (one `DIRT_PROTO`, one `SOLID_PROTO`), never per-call allocations, or a full-zone render/physics sweep GC-thrashes.
- **Zone immutability is a documented invariant.** "Stored nowhere" only works because zone tiles are never altered. If a future design makes any zone tile destructible, it MUST add a copy-on-write sparse override (a `Map` keyed `r*W+c` holding just the diffs) at the same time.
- **Flyweight the town terrain.** All inert fill (zone solid, bedrock fill below shallow towns) shares one frozen object. Dirt/stone use copy-on-write: shared frozen singletons in the array, replaced with a fresh mutable `{type, hp}` only when a drill first damages the cell. Intern per-town immediately after each town generates, so the transient peak is one town's worth of mutable objects, not the whole world. This keeps the `.type`/`.hp` API unchanged.
- **`colRegion` is a flat `Int16Array` built once;** per-tile region lookup is an array index, never a scan over the region list.
- **Prefer per-region local arrays** over one giant jagged world row with holes; centralize world-col to (region, local-col) conversion in exactly ONE helper.
- **Distinguish the rules boundary from the ground seam at each town/zone edge.** The safe-versus-danger threshold (marked by the banner) must be a HARD, legible line, never cross-faded (blend scenery, cut hard for rules). But keep the GROUND continuous via a short neutral "no-man's" surface band (cracked earth / ash / rubble) that both towns blend into, so the one shared surface altitude has no glitchy tile seam: town ground -> short decay -> neutral NMZ ground -> short build-up -> next town ground. Give each town a ~one-screen-width safe apron (visibly calm) before its banner, so each crossing is a clean beat: apron -> warning line -> gauntlet -> next apron.
- **Audit the "one world, one center, one bottom" constants.** `COLS`, `DECK_CENTER_COL`, and especially a single global `BEDROCK_ROW` all encode the old single-town world; each becomes region/town-aware (a single global bedrock floor directly fights "each town drills deeper").

See the project memory entry `project-world-combat-expansion` for the condensed version.

## 4. Working methodology (non-negotiable for this program)

The owner's directive, recorded so every session and agent follows it:

1. **Research before every stage.** Each phase and each non-trivial sub-stage starts by launching focused research agents on the relevant physics and on how the best video games ever made solve this exact problem. Name specific games. Bring back concrete, buildable findings, then implement.
2. **One thing at a time, with full focus.** Decompose into the smallest sensible stages. Good output comes from the model concentrating on a single concern, stopping to think, and researching. Do not bundle unrelated concerns into one stage.
3. **Maximize parallelism where safe.** Research agents run in parallel, always. Implementation runs in parallel only across **distinct** `js/sluice/` fragments (concurrent agents on the same fragment clobber; never edit the generated `js/sluice.js`). See the Parallelization map.
4. **Second-guess regularly. Revisit this plan regularly.** At every phase boundary, re-read this doc, challenge the approach against what the research and the build taught us, and update it.
5. **Deviation is allowed and encouraged**, as long as it follows world-class practice and is research-backed. Record every deviation in the Deviation log.
6. **Opus plans, Sonnet executes.** Architecture, decomposition, and research synthesis are Opus work. Once a stage is well-defined, a Sonnet session carries out the edits.
7. **Version and ship every change.** Edit a fragment (never the bundle), bump `GAME_VERSION` in `000-head.js`, run `./build-sluice.sh`, commit BOTH the fragment(s) and the rebuilt `js/sluice.js`, push to `main`. A change is not done until it is on GitHub.

## 7. Research findings so far

**Combat foundation (2026-05-28 design pass, four parallel agents):**
- **Feel:** auto-turrets remove aiming so the player only flies and dodges; keep jetpack momentum weighty (Luftrausers stall); telegraph everything; lock projectile color by ownership (enemy red/orange, player white/cyan); combine simple enemies into squeezes; pace each crossing intro -> escalate -> peak -> breather.
- **Projectile physics:** semi-implicit Euler; ballistic launch-angle solve (discriminant = range gate); homing via capped-turn-rate steer (default), pure pursuit, or proportional navigation (N 3 to 5, elites), with `maxTurn` as the dodge knob and `maxLife` on everything; quadratic lead/intercept for aimed shots.
- **AI:** one steering skeleton (desired minus current, clamp force, clamp speed) for chasers, strafers, and birds; ground turrets do detect -> LOS -> telegraph -> lead -> fire; birds are cheap Boids or sine paths.
- **Progression:** soft gate with hard-gate readability (warning before damage, retreat window); scale HP/density/new-types hard but per-hit damage gently (Risk of Rain 2 split); armor as percent reduction; price the crossing upgrade above a quick surface grab; let skill punch ~1 tier under-geared.

**P0 architecture pass (2026-05-28, three agents):** two independently validated the region-table + virtual-zone (procedural-on-read) + per-region-rules design as the same approach Terraria/Starbound use, and confirmed tile streaming is not warranted at this scale (the LRU chunk cache already solves the render-cost half). Memory is the one real risk on low-end mobile (uncatchable Safari page ceiling, GPU sims already consuming part of it); the fix is flyweighting (frozen inert + copy-on-write breakables), not a typed-array refactor. See D1 (refined) and the storage invariants in section 3.

**Boundary and world-edge design (2026-05-28, third agent).** Blend scenery, cut hard for rules: the town/NMZ safe-versus-danger line is a HARD legible seam (banner-marked), while the ground stays continuous via a neutral NMZ surface band. Oceans are an illusory horizon (soft bound before the visual edge, water/haze past view, push-back not a wall). Telegraph danger with stacked redundant channels escalating per zone; banners read by silhouette + red-star/era tier before text; give each town a ~one-screen safe apron. Folded into P0 (ocean, seam, apron) and P1/P7 (banners, telegraphing). Sources: Terraria ocean + biome blending, Kingdom Two Crowns hard territory lines, Shinobi background-plane telegraphing, Subnautica dead-zone warnings, pixel-art signage legibility.

Sources captured in the session research (Vlambeer Art of Screenshake, Sparen danmaku guide, proportional-navigation references, projectile-motion and intercept math, Reynolds steering, Risk of Rain 2 difficulty, SteamWorld Dig and Subnautica progression; Terraria/Noita/Starbound/Minecraft world architecture; V8/JSC object-size and mobile-Safari memory ceilings).
